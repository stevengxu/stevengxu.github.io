About

I am a fifth year Ph.D. student studying statistics at North Carolina State University. My thesis advisor is Dr. Brian Reich. My research interests include

  • Bayesian Nonparametrics
  • Causal Inference
  • Anomaly Detection

Specifically, I develop Bayesian non-parametric models for simultaneous estimation of non-crossing, non-linear qauntile curves. An in-depth introduction to my thesis topic can be seen here. I have proposed a Bayesian non-parametric quantile process regression model by combining the approximation power of splines and Neural Network. I am currently working on extending this method to estimate causal quantile effects in precense of many cofounders.

Outside research, I enjoy playing with my two cats Frito and Lilac.
